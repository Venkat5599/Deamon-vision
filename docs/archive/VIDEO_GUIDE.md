# 🎥 Video Guide for Daemon Vision

## Supported Formats

- ✅ **MP4** (H.264/H.265) - RECOMMENDED
- ✅ **AVI** (Any codec)
- ✅ **MOV** (QuickTime)
- ✅ **MKV** (Matroska)

## Detectable Objects

Your system can detect and track:

| Object | Icon | Best Use Case |
|--------|------|---------------|
| Person | 👤 | Pedestrians, crowds, people walking |
| Car | 🚗 | Traffic, parking lots, streets |
| Truck | 🚚 | Highways, loading docks, delivery |
| Bus | 🚌 | Public transport, bus stops |
| Motorcycle | 🏍️ | Traffic, parking areas |
| Bicycle | 🚲 | Bike lanes, parks, streets |
| Airplane | ✈️ | Airports, tarmac, aerial views |

## Video Specifications

### Recommended
- **Resolution**: 1080p or higher (will be resized)
- **Frame Rate**: 30 FPS
- **Duration**: 30-60 seconds (for testing)
- **File Size**: < 500MB
- **Codec**: H.264 (most compatible)

### Minimum
- **Resolution**: 480p
- **Frame Rate**: 15 FPS
- **Duration**: 10 seconds
- **File Size**: Any
- **Codec**: Any OpenCV-compatible

## What Makes a Good Test Video?

### ✅ GOOD
- Multiple objects (3+ cars or people)
- Clear, well-lit scenes
- Objects moving at moderate speed
- Outdoor or well-lit indoor
- Stable camera (not shaky)
- Medium distance (not too close/far)

### ❌ AVOID
- Only 1 object
- Very dark/night scenes
- Extreme close-ups
- Very fast motion blur
- Heavy filters/effects
- Indoor with poor lighting

## Quick Test: Record Your Own

### With Your Phone
1. Go outside to a street or parking area
2. Hold phone steady
3. Record 30 seconds of:
   - Cars driving by, OR
   - People walking, OR
   - Mixed traffic
4. Transfer to computer
5. Upload to Daemon Vision!

### Tips for Recording
- ✅ Hold phone horizontally (landscape)
- ✅ Keep camera steady
- ✅ Record during daytime
- ✅ Include multiple objects
- ✅ Avoid zooming in/out

## Testing Your Video

### Before Uploading
1. Check file format (MP4 recommended)
2. Verify it has detectable objects
3. Make sure it's not too dark
4. Confirm file size < 500MB

### After Uploading
You should see:
- ✅ Bounding boxes around objects
- ✅ Track IDs assigned
- ✅ Confidence scores
- ✅ Objects being tracked across frames
- ✅ FPS counter updating

### If Nothing Detected
- Try a different video with clearer objects
- Ensure good lighting
- Check that objects are in the supported list
- Try a traffic or pedestrian video

## Pro Tips

1. **Start Simple**: Use a traffic video first
2. **Test Different Angles**: Try overhead vs street-level
3. **Mix Objects**: Videos with cars AND people work great
4. **Good Lighting**: Daytime outdoor footage works best
5. **Stable Camera**: Less shaky = better tracking
